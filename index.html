The Problem!

Fast Fashion has been proven to be one of the greatest dangers in our planet.

Textile Waste.

Cheap, trendy clothing is more likely to be thrown out than expensive, classic clothing. This shouldn't come as a surprise because we tend to appreciate things less when we don't spend a lot of money on them. Only 2.5 million tons of the 17 million tons of textile waste produced in 2018 were recycled, according to the Environmental Protection Agency (EPA).1 Compared to other materials like paper, glass, or even plastic, clothing recycles at a much lower rate. This is related to the usage of short-fiber fabrics, which are less expensive, thinner, and incapable of being rewoven into new materials.

CO2 emissions

When clothes are transported from manufacturing to retail locations, carbon emissions happen. Emissions happen once more with each transaction, whether made in person or online. But most importantly, they take place one last time when the product is discarded by the customer, hauled to a landfill, and occasionally burned.

Water Pollution.

These clothes might increase marine pollution. Microplastics can be found in clothing made of synthetic materials. This includes textiles produced from repurposed water bottles, which are frequently promoted as an environmentally beneficial choice. The minute pieces of plastic are flushed into sewer systems and eventually end up in the ocean when these goods are washed or when they are in a landfill and exposed to rain.
